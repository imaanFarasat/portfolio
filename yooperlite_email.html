Scientific Overview of Yooperlite

Chemical Composition:

Syenite rock with fluorescent sodalite and nepheline minerals
Formation Process:

Forms in igneous intrusions through cooling and crystallization of magma
Fluorescent Properties:

Glows bright orange under UV light due to sodalite content
Hardness:

6-7 on the Mohs scale, making it durable for jewelry and carving
Color Variations:

Gray to black base with bright orange fluorescence under UV light
Global Sources:

Found only in the Upper Peninsula of Michigan, USA
